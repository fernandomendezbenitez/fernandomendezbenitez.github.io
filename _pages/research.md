## mathematical & computational finance

**Markets as Markov State Spaces: Greeks, Stochastic Flows, and Reinforcement Learning**

This project develops a rigorous theoretical foundation for treating financial markets as Markov state spaces, with option Greeks serving as latent state variables encoding the local geometry of the pricing measure. Drawing on Kunita's stochastic flow theory and the Malliavin calculus framework of Fourniè et al. (1999), the Greeks are characterised as functionals of the underlying diffusion whose evolution is governed by a system of stochastic differential equations derived from the flow map. Market regimes are classified through summary statistics of the Greek surface — analogues of risk reversal and butterfly — enabling a low-dimensional representation of market state. Reinforcement learning is then deployed as a tractable approximation to the optimal stochastic control policy for hedging and trading, connecting to the Jia-Zhou (2022) framework for continuous-time RL and recent work on neural SDEs as function approximators for value functions.

## computational neuroscience

**Modelling Pain Chronification: A Stochastic Landau-Ginzburg Approach**

In collaboration with researchers in computational neuroscience, this project applies stochastic dynamical systems theory to the problem of pain chronification — the transition from acute to chronic pain. The Landau-Ginzburg phenomenological framework is used to model the bifurcation structure of the nociceptive system, with stochastic perturbations capturing biological noise and inter-individual variability. Reinforcement learning is employed to model maladaptive learning dynamics hypothesised to underlie the persistence of chronic pain states. Simulation results characterise the parameter regimes under which chronification becomes self-sustaining, with implications for clinical intervention timing.
