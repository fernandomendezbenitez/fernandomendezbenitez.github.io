## Overview

Independent research (2025) aimed at resolving the theoretical and practical limitations of the three canonical option-theoretic Discount for Lack of Marketability models — Chaffe, Ghaidarov, and Longstaff — and deriving a hybrid framework with a closed-form analytic approximation.

## Motivation

Each incumbent model imposes restrictive assumptions: Chaffe assumes perfect market timing, while Ghaidarov and Longstaff introduce upside and downside components that produce explosive or poorly-bounded discounts under realistic parameterisations. No existing model simultaneously captures full downside risk and lost upside potential without these pathologies.

## Key Findings

The Hybrid Put framework captures full downside risk and lost upside potential without assuming perfect market timing. It produces robust, non-explosive discounts bounded below by the strictly adverse component, and outperforms all three incumbent models empirically across a broad range of inputs.
